Brief Professional Biography

I have a university education in mathematics and computer science. In summer 2019 I interned in the research department of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, writing code for a project on systemic vulnerabilities in Fedwire, a large-scale financial transaction service. In summer 2018 I was a student researcher with Worcester Polytechnic Institute's financial mathematics REU, working on a project about pricing models for volatility options.

Right now I'm working on a master's thesis under Dr. Alexander Petukhov. The project is to use neural networks for better frame reconstruction, with direct practical applications to preprocessing digital camera output and also film scanners. More broadly, I am interested in harmonic analysis, machine learning, probability theory, and their applications.
